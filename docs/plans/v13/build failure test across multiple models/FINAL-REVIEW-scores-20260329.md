## Conclusion

The gap between the top two and the bottom six is not gradual — it's a cliff. mimo-v2-pro and GPT-5.3-Codex looked at the code and reported what they saw. The other six models, to varying degrees, generated plausible-sounding narratives about what *should* be wrong with a documentation pipeline after a folder refactor, then searched for evidence to fit their theory rather than deriving a theory from evidence. The most telling pattern is the confidence-accuracy inversion: the model that scored 15/100 self-rated "very high diagnostic quality," while the model that scored 97/100 simply listed line numbers and space counts without editorializing. In debugging, the models that show their work are trustworthy; the models that rate their own quality are not. When 75% of your AI diagnosticians fabricate function names, file paths, or platform behaviors, the lesson isn't that AI can't debug — two of them proved it can — but that *most* AI diagnosis output needs to be verified against one `grep` command before you trust a single claim in it.
